## 1. Interest Rate Environment

**How rates affect different sectors:**

| Sector | Rate Rising | Rate Falling |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Financials (banks) | ✅ Net interest margin expands | ❌ Margin compresses |
| Real Estate (REITs) | ❌ Cap rates rise, valuations fall | ✅ Cap rates fall, valuations rise |
| Utilities | ❌ Bond proxy loses appeal | ✅ Dividend yield relatively attractive |
| Growth Tech | ❌ Higher discount rate = multiple compression | ✅ DCF value rises |
| Consumer Staples | Neutral | Neutral |
| Energy | Neutral | Neutral |

**Current rate context questions:**
- Is the Fed in hiking, pausing, or cutting cycle?
- Where are the 10-year Treasury yields?
- What is the yield curve doing (normal, flat, inverted)?

**Inverted yield curve** (short rates > long rates): historically precedes recession by 12-18 months. Bearish for cyclicals, bullish for defensives.

## 2. Economic Cycle Positioning

**Four-phase framework:**

```
EXPANSION → PEAK → CONTRACTION → TROUGH
     ↑                                ↓
     └────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Sector rotation by phase:**

| Phase | Outperforming Sectors | Underperforming |
|-------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Early Expansion | Financials, Consumer Discretionary, Tech | Defensives |
| Late Expansion | Energy, Materials, Industrials | Growth Tech |
| Contraction | Defensives (Utilities, Staples, Healthcare) | Cyclicals |
| Trough/Recovery | Consumer Discretionary, Financials | Defensives |

**Key indicators to watch:**
- ISM Manufacturing PMI: >50 = expansion, <50 = contraction
- Unemployment rate direction (rising = late cycle warning)
- Conference Board Leading Economic Index (LEI)
- Consumer confidence surveys

## 3. Inflation Dynamics

**High inflation environment:**
- Winners: Energy, Materials, Real Assets (pricing power)
- Losers: Bond-proxies, growth stocks with distant earnings, fixed-cost businesses
- Consumer behavior: trading down (benefit to value retail, pressure on premium)

**Deflation/disinflation:**
- Winners: Growth tech (lower rates), Consumer Discretionary (real wage growth)
- Losers: Energy, Commodities, Value cyclicals

**Company-level inflation analysis:**
- Can the company pass through cost increases? (gross margin stability = yes)
- Are input costs rising faster than revenue? (margin compression if yes)
- Does the company have multi-year fixed contracts? (locked out of pricing upside AND downside)

## 4. Dollar Strength

**Strong USD impact:**
- Multinational revenues from overseas worth less in USD terms
- Rule of thumb: 10% USD appreciation ≈ 3-5% headwind to S&P 500 EPS
- Winners: Domestic-focused US companies, importers
- Losers: US exporters, companies with >40% international revenue

**Key check:** What % of revenue is international? Where does the company report currency exposure in its 10-K?

## 6. Credit Market Signals

**Investment grade vs. high yield spreads:**
- Credit spreads widening = risk-off, companies paying more to borrow
- Credit spreads tightening = risk-on, financial conditions easing
- High-yield spread > 600bps = stress territory for leveraged companies

**Leveraged loan market:**
- Floating rate debt = very rate-sensitive for leveraged buyouts
- CLO issuance volume signals institutional appetite for risk

## 7. Macro Overlay Application

**When macro context matters most:**
- Company has >30% revenue exposure to a specific geography
- Business model is rate-sensitive (REIT, bank, insurance, VC-backed)
- Commodity is a major input or output (oil, copper, lumber, silicon)
- Cyclicality is high (autos, housing, capital equipment)

**When macro matters less:**
- Secular growth story overriding economic cycle
- Domestic-only business with pricing power
- Defensive moat (regulated utility, essential software with high switching costs)

**Application for ZEPHYR synthesis:**
- Always establish the macro backdrop in THE SITUATION section
- If macro is a tailwind, say specifically how and quantify if possible
- If macro is a headwind, flag which risk this amplifies
